Rainfall In Australia

Distribution of rainfall in Australia

* Gray areas represent missing data

While a number of Local Government Areas (LGA) have missing data due to difference in rainfall datasource and the LGA map, there are some clear observations that can be made. For example, LGAs further north experience much higher rainfall on average, such as northern Queensland. LGAs at coastal areas also generally receive more rainfall than inland.

Rainfall Patterns over the Years

The seasonal nature of rainfall is displayed here in this spiral chart, each layer represents a year and each dot is a month. There is a clear wet and dry season present where there is excess rainfall from March to June and much less rainfall from September to November.

Monthly Rainfall in Major Cities

This visualisation shows the different seasonal patterns for the major cities in Australia. While Sydney and Brisbane have wet seasons around June, Darwin is the opposite with the most rainfall arriving in December.
